Description:

Turmeric is the dried root of Curcuma longa, a perennial plant from the ginger family. It has a deep golden-yellow color and a warm, bitter flavor. It is widely used for culinary, medicinal, and dyeing purposes.

  • Scientific Name: Curcuma longa
  • Family: Zingiberaceae
  • Origin: Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia
  • Plant Type: Herbaceous perennial
  • Plant Height: 1‚Äì1.5 meters
  • Rhizome Characteristics: Bright yellow-orange interior, rough brown skin
  • Essential Compounds: Curcumin (primary bioactive compound)
  • Soil & Climate: Well-drained, fertile soil with warm and humid climate

Key Characteristics:

  • Color: Bright golden-yellow
  • Form: Rhizome (fresh or dried), ground powder
  • Flavor: Warm, bitter, earthy
  • Aroma: Distinct, pungent, slightly musky

Common Uses:

  • Culinary: Curries, spice blends, pickles, sauces
  • Medicinal: Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, digestive aid
  • Dyeing: Used as natural textile and food dye

Health Benefits:

  • Reduces inflammation and joint pain
  • Boosts immunity and improves digestion
  • May support heart and brain health
  • Rich in antioxidants like curcumin
