🌿 Fenugreek Seed (Trigonella foenum-graecum L.)

📝 Description:

Fenugreek seeds are small, yellowish-brown in color, and have a slightly bitter, nutty flavor 🌰. They’re a versatile spice used in Indian cuisine, herbal remedies, and even cosmetics.

🍽️ Culinary & Medicinal Uses:

  • A key ingredient in spice blends, pickles, and curries
  • Fresh fenugreek leaves (methi) are used as a leafy green vegetable
  • Widely used in Ayurveda and traditional medicine

💚 Health Benefits:

  • 🌡️ Helps regulate blood sugar
  • 🧘 Aids in digestion
  • 🔥 Contains anti-inflammatory compounds like saponins and alkaloids

🌱 Botanical Profile of Fenugreek

  • Scientific Name: Trigonella foenum-graecum
  • Family: Fabaceae (Leguminosae)
  • Origin: Mediterranean & Southwest Asia
  • Plant Type: Annual herb
  • Height: 30–60 cm
  • Leaves: Trifoliate, light green
  • Flowers: Small, pale yellow or white
  • Seeds: Small, oblong, yellow-brown, aromatic
  • Active Compounds: Saponins, alkaloids (trigonelline), diosgenin

🪴 Growing Conditions:

  • Soil: Loamy, well-drained
  • Climate: Cool, dry
  • Sowing Time: October–November
  • Harvest: February–March

🌟 Popular Fenugreek Varieties in India

  • RMt-1: 1,300–1,500 kg/ha yield, 140–150 days, moderately disease-resistant
  • RMt-305: 1,400–1,700 kg/ha, 150–160 days, fungal disease tolerant
  • Gujarat Methi-1: 1,200–1,400 kg/ha, 140–150 days, powdery mildew resistance
  • Gujarat Methi-2: 1,300–1,600 kg/ha, 145–155 days, downy mildew & wilt resistance
  • Hisar Sonali: 1,500–1,800 kg/ha, 155–165 days, resistant to wilt & rust
  • Hisar Madhavi: 1,400–1,700 kg/ha, 150–160 days, powdery mildew tolerant
  • Pusa Early Bunching: 1,100–1,300 kg/ha, 120–130 days (early), cold-tolerant
  • CO-1: 1,200–1,500 kg/ha, 140–150 days, fusarium wilt tolerant
