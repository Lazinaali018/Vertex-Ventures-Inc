Description:

Cumin is the dried seed of Cuminum cyminum, a herbaceous annual plant from the Apiaceae family. Known for its strong, earthy flavor and aromatic profile, cumin is widely used in culinary, medicinal, and essential oil applications.

  • Scientific Name: Cuminum cyminum
  • Family: Apiaceae (Umbelliferae)
  • Origin: Mediterranean region
  • Plant Type: Herbaceous annual
  • Plant Height: 30‚Äì50 cm
  • Seed Characteristics: Elongated, ridged, light brown
  • Essential Oil Content: 2.5‚Äì5% (rich in cuminaldehyde)
  • Soil & Climate: Sandy loam soil, cool and dry climate
  • Sowing Time: October‚ÄìNovember
  • Harvesting: February‚ÄìMarch

Key Characteristics:

  • Color: Light to yellowish-brown
  • Shape: Elongated, ridged seeds
  • Flavor: Warm, earthy, slightly bitter
  • Aroma: Strong and aromatic due to cuminaldehyde

Common Uses:

  • Culinary: Used in spice blends, curries, soups, and stews
  • Medicinal: Promotes digestion and relieves inflammation
  • Essential Oils: Used in perfumes and traditional medicines

Health Benefits:

  • Improves digestion and reduces bloating
  • Excellent source of iron
  • Rich in antioxidants
