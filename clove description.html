Description:

Clove is the dried flower bud of the clove tree (Syzygium aromaticum), a member of the Myrtaceae family. Known for its warm, pungent aroma and intense flavor, it is one of the most prized spices in the world. Cloves are used extensively in cooking, traditional medicine, and aromatics.

  • Scientific Name: Syzygium aromaticum
  • Family: Myrtaceae
  • Origin: Indonesia (Maluku Islands ‚Äì ‚ÄúThe Spice Islands‚Äù)
  • Major Producers: Indonesia, India, Sri Lanka, Madagascar, Tanzania
  • Essential Oil Content: 15‚Äì20% (rich in eugenol)
  • Harvesting: Buds are harvested before blooming, 4‚Äì5 years after planting

Key Characteristics:

  • Color: Ranges from dark brown to reddish-brown
  • Shape: Small, nail-like dried flower buds
  • Flavor: Warm, spicy, slightly bitter
  • Aroma: Strong and penetrating due to high eugenol content

Common Uses:

  • Culinary: A staple in spice blends, teas, baked goods, and curries
  • Medicinal: Known for antiseptic, analgesic, and antibacterial properties
  • Cosmetic & Aromatic: Found in perfumes, toothpaste, and essential oil products

Health Benefits:

  • Rich in eugenol, a powerful anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving compound
  • Supports dental health, relieves toothaches
  • Aids in digestion and improves metabolism
